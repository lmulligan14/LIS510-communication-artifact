Admissions Security Breach

How to protect yourself and your data

Was I affected?

On March 4th, 2019, phishing emails were sent to admissions officers at Grinnell College, from which an unauthorized party obtained security credentials allowing them to access the admissions databases stored on the web service Slate.

While the full extent of the breach is still under investigation, it is believed that admissions data from applicants going as far back as the 2014 fall semester may have been obtained. Those whose information may have been potentially compromised have been contacted either through email or social media with more information.

How can I protect myself?

According to our findings admission files containing student academic records, admissions officer comments, and admissions decisions were obtained. It is also possible that financial creditentials were accessed.
Here are some things you can do:
  • Be wary of suspicious emails regarding the purchasing of Grinell or other universities admissions information.
  • Monitor your finincial accounts for suspicious activity.
  • Change all passwords associated with your Grinnell admissions account, or similar passwords on other accounts.
  • Contact our admissions office to find out if you are at risk and what information of yours may have been obtained.
